# Task 5: Reflection and Assessment (Metacognitive Integration)

## Overview
This final task focuses on reflection, self-assessment, and evaluation of learning processes across the hybrid writing cycle.

It integrates outcomes from Tasks 1–4, enabling learners to critically analyse their development in L2 Chinese writing through manual, phonological, situated, and multimodal dimensions.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this task, learners will be able to:

- Reflect on their writing development across modalities
- Identify strengths and weaknesses in their learning process
- Evaluate their use of pinyin, hanzi, and multimodal resources
- Demonstrate awareness of learning strategies and revision processes

---

## Materials

- Outputs from Tasks 1–4
- Reflection prompts or journal template
- Assessment rubrics (self/peer/teacher)
- Digital or paper reflection format

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: Portfolio Review
- Collect all outputs from previous tasks
- Organise them chronologically

---

### Step 2: Guided Reflection
Respond to prompts such as:
- What changed in your writing from Task 1 to Task 4?
- Which modality was most difficult and why?
- How did pinyin help or interfere with writing?
- How did AR or multimodal tasks change your learning?

---

### Step 3: Self-Assessment
- Evaluate performance using rubric criteria:
  - orthographic accuracy
  - phonological awareness
  - multimodal integration
  - revision quality

---

### Step 4: Peer Assessment
- Exchange reflections with peers
- Provide structured feedback

---

### Step 5: Synthesis
- Write final integrative reflection
- Identify main learning strategies used

---

## Outputs

- Reflective journal
- Self-assessment rubric
- Peer feedback notes
- Final synthesis text

---

## Research Layer (Data Generation)

This task generates data on:

- Metacognitive awareness development
- Learner self-regulation strategies
- Perceived difficulty across modalities
- Alignment between performance and perception
- Longitudinal learning trajectories

---

## Analytical Use

This dataset supports:

- Triangulation of cognitive + behavioural + reflective data
- Analysis of learner autonomy development
- Validation of hybrid writing model
- Integration of subjective and performative evidence

---

## Pedagogical–Research Integration

In this task:

- Reflection = data source
- Self-assessment = analytical layer
- Metacognition = observable construct

---

## Notes for Teachers / Researchers

This task closes the hybrid writing cycle and enables full traceability of learning across modalities. It is essential for linking pedagogical intervention with research outcomes.
