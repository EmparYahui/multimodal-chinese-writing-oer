# Task 3: Situated Multimodal Production (AR-supported Fieldwork)

## Overview
This task situates L2 Chinese learning in real-world environments through augmented reality (AR), fieldwork observation, and multimodal data collection.

It extends previous tasks by embedding writing practices in spatial, visual, and interactional contexts.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this task, learners will be able to:

- Use Chinese in real-world contextual settings
- Collect and annotate multimodal data (image, audio, text)
- Link linguistic forms to spatial referents
- Develop situated lexical and cultural awareness

---

## Materials

- Mobile device (smartphone/tablet)
- AR-enabled application or mapping tool (e.g., Google Maps)
- Camera/audio recorder
- Fieldwork notebook or digital log
- Task 1 and Task 2 outputs

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: Fieldwork Preparation
- Review target vocabulary related to urban space
- Plan a route in the designated area (e.g., Chinatown or cultural district)

---

### Step 2: Environmental Observation
- Identify linguistic and cultural elements in the environment
- Take photos, videos, and audio recordings

---

### Step 3: AR Annotation
- Add Chinese captions, labels, or descriptions to geolocated points
- Link language forms to real-world objects or spaces

---

### Step 4: Multimodal Logging
- Record brief descriptions in pinyin or hanzi
- Combine visual + textual + audio data

---

### Step 5: Peer Interaction
- Share findings with peers via messaging or group platform
- Discuss lexical choices and contextual interpretations

---

### Step 6: Reflection
- How does real-world context affect vocabulary selection?
- What difficulties arise when mapping language to space?

---

## Outputs

- Geolocated annotated artefacts
- Photos/videos with Chinese captions
- Audio recordings with written transcripts
- Fieldwork log
- Reflection note

---

## Research Layer (Data Generation)

This task produces multimodal empirical data including:

- Contextualised lexical usage
- Spatial-linguistic alignment patterns
- Learner-generated AR annotations
- Interactional negotiation of meaning
- Multimodal composition traces

---

## Analytical Use

This dataset supports:

- Study of situated language use in L2 Chinese
- Analysis of spatialised meaning-making
- Comparison with non-situated writing tasks (Tasks 1–2)
- Multimodal discourse analysis (DMC framework)

---

## Pedagogical–Research Integration

In this task:

- Space = linguistic input
- Observation = data generation
- Annotation = writing practice
- Fieldwork = cognitive + social activation

---

## Notes for Teachers / Researchers

This task is designed to bridge classroom learning with real-world linguistic landscapes. It operationalises augmented reality as a mediational tool for situated literacy development rather than as an add-on technology.
