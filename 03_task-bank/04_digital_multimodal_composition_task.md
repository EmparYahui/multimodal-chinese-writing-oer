# Task 4: Digital Multimodal Composition (DMC)

## Overview
This task focuses on the integration of linguistic, visual, and digital resources to produce multimodal texts in L2 Chinese.

It builds on manual writing (Task 1), pinyin mediation (Task 2), and situated fieldwork (Task 3), shifting towards structured multimodal authorship.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this task, learners will be able to:

- Integrate text, image, and audio in coherent digital artefacts
- Use Chinese (hanzi/pinyin) in multimodal digital environments
- Revise and reorganise multimodal content for clarity and coherence
- Develop awareness of audience and communicative purpose

---

## Materials

- Mobile device or computer
- Editing tools (video, audio, image annotation, subtitling software)
- Outputs from Tasks 1–3
- Multimodal templates or platforms (e.g., maps, social media, presentations)

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: Content Selection
- Review materials collected in previous tasks
- Select relevant images, texts, and audio fragments

---

### Step 2: Draft Composition
- Create initial multimodal narrative (text + image + audio)
- Use Chinese for captions, subtitles, or annotations

---

### Step 3: Structuring and Editing
- Organise content into coherent sequence
- Adjust linguistic accuracy (hanzi/pinyin)
- Align multimodal elements (visual-text-audio consistency)

---

### Step 4: Peer Review
- Exchange drafts with peers
- Provide feedback on clarity, language use, and multimodal coherence

---

### Step 5: Revision
- Incorporate feedback
- Refine linguistic and visual integration

---

### Step 6: Final Publication
- Export or publish final multimodal artefact (guide, video, map, presentation)

---

## Outputs

- Multimodal digital artefact (video / guide / map / presentation)
- Chinese captions or subtitles
- Revision log
- Peer feedback notes
- Final reflection

---

## Research Layer (Data Generation)

This task generates data on:

- Multimodal integration strategies
- Revision patterns in digital composition
- Alignment between linguistic and visual modes
- Learner decisions in semiotic design
- Interaction between prior tasks and digital output

---

## Analytical Use

This dataset enables:

- Study of multimodal literacy development
- Analysis of DMC processes in L2 Chinese
- Comparison of analogue vs digital writing trajectories
- Examination of revision as semiotic redesign

---

## Pedagogical–Research Integration

In this task:

- Composition = learning process
- Digital artefact = research corpus
- Revision = observable cognitive and semiotic restructuring

---

## Notes for Teachers / Researchers

This task operationalises digital multimodal composing as a core literacy practice rather than a supplementary activity. It is intended to bridge linguistic learning with real-world digital authorship.
