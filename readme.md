# Multimodal Chinese Writing OER (A1–A2)

This Open Educational Resource (OER) presents a pedagogical framework for beginner Chinese as a second language (A1–A2), integrating hybrid writing (manual + pinyin), digital multimodal composing (DMC), and augmented reality (AR)-supported situated learning.

The resource is designed for classroom implementation, adaptation, and reuse in L2 Chinese contexts.

---

## Core Pedagogical Principles

### 1. Hybrid Writing
Writing as a distributed cognitive process combining:
- manual hanzi production
- pinyin mediation
- digital revision and restructuring

### 2. Digital Multimodal Composing (DMC)
Writing as semiotic design integrating:
- text
- image
- audio
- digital platforms

### 3. Augmented Reality & Situated Learning
Language learning embedded in real-world contexts through:
- geolocated tasks
- field observation
- experiential meaning-making

---

## Pedagogical Structure

The learning design follows a four-phase cycle:

1. Encoding (manual writing)
2. Mediation (pinyin–hanzi mapping)
3. Expansion (multimodal + AR tasks)
4. Reflection (revision and metacognitive consolidation)

---

## What this OER provides

- Task sequence for A1–A2 Chinese learners
- Hybrid writing activities (manual + digital)
- Multimodal composing tasks
- AR-supported fieldwork design
- Revision and error-awareness toolkit
